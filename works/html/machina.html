Machina, Anima


2025

Blender

The musical visualizer explores the following inquiry: If a machine could listen to the same piece like humans, how would it interpret it, and what would that experience look like?


Source: Symphony No. 8 in E-Flat Major "Symphony of a Thousand", Zweiter Teil: XV. Alles Vergängliche ist nur ein Gleichnis, by Gustav Mahler.

Today, computer technology is widely used as a tool that expands the artist's visual world. In many cases, this integration fosters new inspirations and creativity. From assisting in design to generating complex visual compositions, technologies such as AI and digital algorithms allow artists to explore new forms of expression that were once unimaginable.

My inquriy into the intersection between technology and art began after listening to a musical composition by Gustav Mahler. The orchestral masterpiece served as a powerful reminder of the human ingenuity—the ability to convey something grand and universal through sound alone. This experience led me to wonder: If a machine could listen to the same piece, how would it interpret it? What would that experience look like?

The visuals of Machina, Anima serve to reflect the question. It features three dynamic elements that resemble living organisms, despite being entirely composed of computer-generated graphics. Their shifting forms respond to different parts of the music, giving the impression that the machine is "listening" and "reacting". The goal of this piece, is to invite viewers to consider the relationship between artifical systems and human expression—blurring the line between programmed responses and organic perception.
